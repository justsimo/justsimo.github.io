About me

Something About Me

I care about one thing above all: AI systems that are measurable, reliable and actually useful outside the lab, not just on benchmarks. My work spans applied AI systems, from NLP pipelines to document intelligence, and my research in Machine Unlearning keeps me honest about what happens to data and to people once a model has learned it. Responsible AI is not a buzzword for me, it is a design constraint.

Away from the code, I co-founded a university esports community with 100+ members and a Red Bull partnership, led the AURORA Student Association for six years, and served in the National Civil Service for digital inclusion, bringing the same people-first mindset to everything I build.

Research

Machine Unlearning: A Perspective, Taxonomy, and Benchmark Evaluation

Future Internet | MDPI | 2026

Simone Gatto, Cristian Cosentino, Fabrizio Marozzo, Pietro Liò

A peer-reviewed paper on Machine Unlearning, proposing a taxonomy and benchmark evaluation for supervised NLP scenarios.

The work evaluates retraining, SISA and approximate unlearning methods on DistilBERT/AG News across utility, forgetting, privacy and efficiency.

🤖 Arithmetic Reasoning with Transformers

Research project investigating whether Transformers can learn to compute the Greatest Common Divisor as a supervised sequence-to-sequence task.

Designed tokenisation schemes across multiple bases, tested uniform and log-uniform training distributions, and evaluated accuracy, grokking dynamics and out-of-distribution generalisation on natural and stratified test sets.
